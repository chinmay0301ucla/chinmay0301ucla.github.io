What is the vision for the dataset?

We see this dataset as a first attempt to galvanize research effort towards the fledgling domain of AI and physics for vision. We hope that the dataset provides a direction for the development of algorithms towards the larger goal of discovering unknown physics from videos.

Why are all the tasks in the dataset elementary?

For our first attempt, we limit the physics tasks to simple, motion dependent tasks. These tasks are well known yet fundamental, with well-defined, closed form physics equations. They are therefore ideal for setting up initial approaches towards the greater effort of physics discovery.


How robust is the dataset to scale changes/camera motion effects between videos?

Active efforts have been taken to ensure that the camera moves minimally between all the videos for a particular task. While small motions do invariably creep in, these motions are limited such that the scale of the object of interest does not change.


What kind of variations are included in the data?

The data is collected so that the governing parameters for the relevant tasks (initial velocities for the ball toss, spring constant and mass for the spring dataset and so on) are well spread, in order to ensure that the dataset is overall more representative.
