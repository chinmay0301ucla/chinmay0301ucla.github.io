Visual Physics

Dataset

The Visual Physics dataset is a repository of real-world videos, capturing simple yet fundamental physics phenomena in practical and experimental scenarios. Through this dataset, we look to motivate research towards the discovery of (eventually unknown) physics phenomena from videos.

This dataset is a part of the larger effort to serve as a testbed for machines to learn natural laws from data, as opposed to standard prediction tasks. Our motivation stems from the famous story of the apple falling on newton's head that led him to discover gravity. Coming up with algorithms capable of inferring and using latent system dynamics of a system through visual data would be a crucial step in the quest to achieve general intelligence, and this dataset aims to be a stepping stone towards that.

Our dataset consists of fundamental high school physics tasks, which are governed by closed form equations. We hope that this dataset would inspire the development of algorithms capable of discovering these fundamental laws of physics with minimal knowledge and intervention. To motivate this goal, we are hosting a challenge to evaluate the performance of the algorithms on this dataset. You can have a look at the submission details and evaluation metrics on the leaderboard.

Our Results


Our method learns parameters strongly related to the initial velocities along the x and y directions in an unsupervised manner. These embeddings are then used to identify governing equations for the physics at hand. As expected, equations very similar to the well known kinematic equations are learnt.
